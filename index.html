About me

Ed-tech keeps solving for access. After a decade scaling STEM content at zyBooks and now Learnvia, I've come to think the real bottleneck is the craft of explaining a particular idea to a particular student at the moment they need it. The work I'm trying to do is use AI to scale that craft, not flatten it into the generic feedback students learn to ignore.

At Learnvia, originally formed as a collaboration between Carnegie Mellon University and the Gates Foundation, I lead the team that's testing whether AI can do this. Before that, I led math, statistics, and data science content at Wiley/zyBooks, where I grew the line 5x in five years.

These books are the proof of work. Each one is an attempt at courseware that explains rather than just assesses, and treats worked examples and feedback as first-class features instead of afterthoughts:

  • Machine Learning, the first ML courseware on the zyBooks/Wiley platform
  • Statistics for Decision Making (lead author)
  • Data Science Foundations (Python) / (R) / (Non-programming)
  • Linear Algebra (lead author)
  • Applied Statistics with Data Analytics (Python) / (R) (lead author)

I also teach math and statistics at Austin Community College, and I actually like doing it. ACC students are the very group we're trying to help at Learnvia, so teaching them every week keeps me honest about whether my ideas about content really hold up. On the side, I run Season Learning, a tutoring company that keeps me close to students earlier in the same pipeline.
