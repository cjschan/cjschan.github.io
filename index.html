About me

Ed-tech keeps solving for access. After a decade scaling STEM content — first at zyBooks, now at Learnvia — I've come to think the real bottleneck is the craft of explaining a particular idea to a particular student at the moment they need it. The work I'm trying to do is use AI to scale that craft, not flatten it into the generic feedback students learn to ignore.

Today, I lead content at Learnvia, originally formed as a collaboration between Carnegie Mellon University and the Gates Foundation. Before that, I was Director of Content Development at John Wiley & Sons, where I managed the mathematics, statistics, and data science titles on the zyBooks platform.

These books are the proof of work — each one an attempt at courseware that explains rather than just assesses, and treats worked examples and feedback as first-class features instead of afterthoughts:

  • Machine Learning
  • Statistics for Decision Making
  • Data Science Foundations (Python) / (R) / (Non-programming)
  • Linear Algebra
  • Applied Statistics with Data Analytics (Python) / (R)

I also teach math and statistics at Austin Community College, and I actually like doing it. ACC students are the very group we're trying to help at Learnvia, so teaching them every week keeps me honest about whether my ideas about content really hold up. On the side, I run Season Learning, a tutoring company that keeps me close to students earlier in the same pipeline.
