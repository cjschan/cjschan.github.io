Quiz 2

Categorical and Quantitative Data: Summary Statistics

Worked Solutions

Covers Sections 2.1, 2.2, 2.3

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Total: 10 points

Problem 1 [2 pts]

Find the mean and the median of \(4, 7, 7, 8, 9, 11, 14, 20\). Which is more affected if we replace 20 by 200? (1 mean, 0.5 median, 0.5 explain)

Mean: \(\frac{4+7+7+8+9+11+14+20}{8} = \frac{80}{8} = 10\).

Median: with \(n = 8\) (even), average the 4th and 5th values: \(\frac{8+9}{2} = 8.5\).

Replace 20 by 200: new sum \(= 80 - 20 + 200 = 260\), so the new mean \(= \frac{260}{8} = 32.5\) (jumps from 10 to 32.5). The middle two values are still 8 and 9, so the median stays \(8.5\).

The mean is more affected. The mean uses every value, so a single outlier pulls it strongly; the median depends only on the middle position and is resistant to outliers.

Problem 2 [2 pts]

Use the histogram below — body-mass index (BMI) of 60 patients — to estimate the median and describe the shape. (1 pt each)

Median: the center of the distribution falls at a BMI of about \(27\). Reading the histogram, roughly half the patients lie below \(\approx 27\) and half above, so the median is near \(27\).

Shape: the distribution is right-skewed (skewed toward high values). Most patients cluster in the low-to-mid 20s, and there is a long tail stretching out toward the larger BMI values (40, 42, 45). Because of that right tail, the mean would sit a bit above the median.

Problem 3 [2 pts]

For \(4, 6, 7, 9, 10, 14\), compute the standard deviation by hand. Show your steps.

Step 1 — mean: \(\bar{x} = \frac{4+6+7+9+10+14}{6} = \frac{50}{6} = 8.3\overline{3}\).

Step 2 — deviations and squared deviations (using \(\bar{x} = 8.333\)):

  • \(4 - 8.333 = -4.333,\ (-4.333)^2 = 18.778\)
  • \(6 - 8.333 = -2.333,\ (-2.333)^2 = 5.444\)
  • \(7 - 8.333 = -1.333,\ (-1.333)^2 = 1.778\)
  • \(9 - 8.333 = 0.667,\ (0.667)^2 = 0.444\)
  • \(10 - 8.333 = 1.667,\ (1.667)^2 = 2.778\)
  • \(14 - 8.333 = 5.667,\ (5.667)^2 = 32.111\)

Step 3 — sum of squares: \(18.778 + 5.444 + 1.778 + 0.444 + 2.778 + 32.111 = 61.333\).

Step 4 — divide by \(n-1 = 5\): \(s^2 = \frac{61.333}{5} = 12.267\).

Step 5 — square root: \(s = \sqrt{12.267} \approx 3.50\).

Problem 4 [2 pts]

A survey asked 200 students their class standing and whether they prefer online or in-person classes. The results are below. (1 pt each)

OnlineIn-Person
Freshman3020
Sophomore2525
Junior1535
Senior1040
  1. What proportion of all surveyed students prefer online classes?
  2. Which class standing has the highest proportion preferring online classes?

(a) Total preferring online \(= 30 + 25 + 15 + 10 = 80\) out of \(200\) students, so the proportion is \(\frac{80}{200} = 0.40\) (40%).

(b) Compute the online proportion within each row (each class standing has \(50\) students):

  • Freshman: \(\frac{30}{50} = 0.60\)
  • Sophomore: \(\frac{25}{50} = 0.50\)
  • Junior: \(\frac{15}{50} = 0.30\)
  • Senior: \(\frac{10}{50} = 0.20\)

Freshmen have the highest proportion preferring online classes at \(0.60\) (60%).

Problem 5 [2 pts]

Two data sets each have a mean of 50. Data set A has a standard deviation of 3 and data set B has a standard deviation of 12. Assume both are roughly bell-shaped. (0.5 + 1.5)

  1. Which data set has more variability?
  2. Using the 95% rule, give the range that captures about 95% of the values for each data set.

(a) Data set B has more variability, since its standard deviation (\(12\)) is larger than A's (\(3\)).

(b) The 95% rule says about 95% of values fall within \(\bar{x} \pm 2 \cdot \text{SD}\).

  • Data set A: \(50 \pm 2(3) = 50 \pm 6\), i.e. from \(44\) to \(56\).
  • Data set B: \(50 \pm 2(12) = 50 \pm 24\), i.e. from \(26\) to \(74\).
