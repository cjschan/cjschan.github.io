Quiz 3

Two Quantitative Variables: Correlation and Regression

Worked Solutions

Covers Sections 2.5, 2.6

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Total: 10 points

Problem 1 [2 pts]

Which scatterplot would correspond to a correlation of \(r \approx -0.85\)? Justify your choice.

  1. Points lie nearly on a line going up to the right.
  2. Points lie nearly on a line going down to the right.
  3. Points form a wide cloud with no clear direction.
  4. Points form a U-shape.

Choice (b). A correlation of \(r \approx -0.85\) is strong (close to \(\pm 1\)) and negative. The sign tells us the direction: as \(x\) increases, \(y\) tends to decrease, so the points fall down to the right. The magnitude near 1 means the points cluster tightly around a straight line.

Why not the others: (a) is a strong positive trend (\(r \approx +0.85\)); (c) is a weak/near-zero correlation (\(r \approx 0\)); (d) is a curved (nonlinear) pattern, for which \(r\) does not describe the relationship well and could be near 0 even though the variables are related.

Problem 2 [3 pts]

A least-squares regression of exam score on hours studied gives \(\hat{y} = 50 + 4x\), where \(x\) = hours studied. (1 pt each)

  1. Interpret the slope in context.
  2. Predict the exam score for a student who studies 6 hours.
  3. Should you use this line to predict the score for a student who studies 40 hours? Explain.

(a) The slope is 4. For each additional hour studied, the predicted exam score increases by 4 points, on average.

(b) \(\hat{y} = 50 + 4(6) = 50 + 24 = 74\). The predicted exam score is 74 points.

(c) No. Studying 40 hours is far outside the range of the data used to build the model (extrapolation). The linear relationship is not guaranteed to hold there, and a score cannot exceed 100 anyway, so the prediction would be unreliable.

Problem 3 [3 pts]

A researcher collects data on the number of hours of sunlight per day (\(x\)) and the height of tomato plants in centimeters (\(y\)) after 30 days. The regression equation is \(\hat{y} = 5.2 + 3.8x\). (1 pt each)

  1. Interpret the slope in context.
  2. Predict the height of a tomato plant that received 6 hours of sunlight.
  3. That plant actually grew to 31 cm. Compute the residual.

(a) The slope is 3.8. For each additional hour of sunlight per day, the predicted height of a tomato plant increases by 3.8 cm, on average.

(b) \(\hat{y} = 5.2 + 3.8(6) = 5.2 + 22.8 = 28\) cm. The predicted height is 28 cm.

(c) The residual is observed minus predicted: \(31 - 28 = +3\) cm. The plant grew 3 cm taller than the line predicted.

Problem 4 [2 pts]

True or false? Give a brief explanation for each. (1 pt each)

  1. If \(r = 0.95\) between shoe size and reading level in elementary school children, then bigger shoes cause better reading.
  2. If we convert the \(x\)-variable from inches to centimeters, the correlation \(r\) will change.

(a) False. Correlation does not imply causation. Here a lurking variable, age, drives both: older children have bigger feet and read better. Buying bigger shoes would not improve reading.

(b) False. Correlation is unitless and is unaffected by a linear change of units. Converting inches to centimeters (multiplying by a positive constant) leaves \(r\) exactly the same.
