Quiz 8

Paired Differences, Chi-Square, and ANOVA

Worked Solutions

Covers Sections 6.5, 7.2, 8.1

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Total: 10 points

Problem 1 [2 pts]

For each design, decide whether you should use a paired or an independent two-sample approach. (1 pt each)

  1. Measure 30 patients' blood pressure before and after a treatment.
  2. Compare cholesterol levels in 30 patients on Drug A and 30 different patients on Drug B.

(a) Paired (each patient is measured twice; differences cancel patient-level variation).

(b) Independent two-sample (different patients in each group).

Problem 2 [2 pts]

A weight-loss program enrolls 10 participants. Each is weighed at the start and after 8 weeks. The differences (start − end, in lbs) for the 10 participants have mean \(\bar{d} = 5\) and \(s_d = 4\). Construct an approximate 95% CI for the mean weight loss using \(\bar{d} \pm 2 \cdot s_d/\sqrt{n}\). (2 pts)

\(SE = 4/\sqrt{10} \approx 1.265\). CI: \(5 \pm 2(1.265) = 5 \pm 2.53\), or approximately \((2.47,\ 7.53)\) lbs.

Problem 3 [2 pts]

A 2 × 3 contingency table has overall total 200, row totals 80 and 120, and column totals 60, 80, 60. Compute the expected count in row 1, column 1 under independence using \(E = (\text{row total} \cdot \text{col total}) / N\). (2 pts)

\(E_{11} = (80 \cdot 60) / 200 = 4800/200 = 24\).

Problem 4 [2 pts]

A chi-square test of independence on the table from Problem 3 yields \(\chi^2 = 12.4\) with df = 2 and p-value ≈ 0.002. State the conclusion at \(\alpha = 0.05\) and interpret it in context (assume rows = political party, columns = favorite news source). (1 + 1)

Reject \(H_0\). There is significant evidence that political party and news source preference are not independent (i.e., they are associated).
