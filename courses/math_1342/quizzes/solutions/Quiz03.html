Quiz 3

Two Quantitative Variables and Probability

Worked Solutions

Covers Sections 2.5, 2.6, P.1

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Total: 10 points

Problem 1 [2 pts]

Which scatterplot would correspond to a correlation of \(r \approx -0.85\)? Justify your choice.

  1. Points lie nearly on a line going up to the right.
  2. Points lie nearly on a line going down to the right.
  3. Points form a wide cloud with no clear direction.
  4. Points form a U-shape.

(b). \(r \approx -0.85\) means strong, negative, linear association. (a) is strongly positive, (c) near zero, (d) near zero (curved).

Problem 2 [3 pts]

A least-squares regression of exam score on hours studied gives \(\hat{y} = 50 + 4x\), where \(x\) = hours studied. (1 pt each)

  1. Interpret the slope in context.
  2. Predict the exam score for a student who studies 6 hours.
  3. Should you use this line to predict the score for a student who studies 40 hours? Explain.

(a) For each additional hour studied, the predicted exam score increases by 4 points.

(b) \(\hat{y} = 50 + 4(6) = 74\).

(c) No — predicting at 40 hours would be extrapolation far outside the range of the data, which is unreliable.

Problem 3 [3 pts]

A jar holds 10 red, 6 blue, and 4 green marbles. Drawing one at random, find: (1 pt each)

  1. \(P(\text{red})\)
  2. \(P(\text{red or green})\)
  3. If two marbles are drawn without replacement, find \(P(\text{both blue})\).

Total = 20. (a) \(\frac{10}{20} = 0.5\).

(b) Disjoint: \(\frac{10}{20} + \frac{4}{20} = \frac{14}{20} = 0.7\).

(c) \(\frac{6}{20} \cdot \frac{5}{19} = \frac{30}{380} = \frac{3}{38} \approx 0.079\).

Problem 4 [2 pts]

An admissions office finds that 60% of applicants are in-state and 40% are out-of-state. Among in-state applicants, 30% receive a merit scholarship; among out-of-state applicants, 20% receive one. If a randomly selected applicant has a merit scholarship, what is the probability they are in-state? (2 pts)

\(P(\text{in-state and merit}) = 0.60 \cdot 0.30 = 0.18\). \(P(\text{out-of-state and merit}) = 0.40 \cdot 0.20 = 0.08\). \(P(\text{merit}) = 0.18 + 0.08 = 0.26\).

\(P(\text{in-state} \mid \text{merit}) = \frac{0.18}{0.26} \approx 0.692\).
