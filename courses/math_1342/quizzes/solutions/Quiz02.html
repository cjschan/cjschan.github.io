Experiments and Descriptive Statistics

Worked Solutions

Covers Sections 1.3, 2.1, 2.2

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Total: 10 points

Problem 1 [2 pts]

A researcher runs an experiment to test whether a new fertilizer increases tomato yield. Each plot is randomly assigned either the new fertilizer or the standard one. Identify the explanatory variable, the response variable, and what makes this an experiment (rather than an observational study). (0.5 + 0.5 + 1)

Explanatory: type of fertilizer (categorical). Response: tomato yield (quantitative). It is an experiment because the researcher randomly assigns the treatments rather than just observing what each plot already received.

Problem 2 [2 pts]

Among 250 surveyed customers, 90 chose vanilla, 120 chose chocolate, and 40 chose strawberry. Compute the proportion that chose each flavor. (0.5 pt each + 0.5 pt setup)

Total = 250. Vanilla: \(90/250 = 0.36\). Chocolate: \(120/250 = 0.48\). Strawberry: \(40/250 = 0.16\). (Sum = 1.00 ✓.)

Problem 3 [2 pts]

Find the mean and the median of the data set: \(4, 7, 7, 8, 9, 11, 14, 20\). Which is more affected if we replace 20 by 200? (1 pt mean, 0.5 pt median, 0.5 pt explain)

Mean = \(80/8 = 10\). Median = average of 4th and 5th values = \((8+9)/2 = 8.5\).

Replacing 20 with 200 changes the mean to \(260/8 = 32.5\) but leaves the median unchanged at 8.5. The mean is far more affected by the outlier.

Problem 4 [2 pts]

Describe the shape of each distribution from its histogram features.

  1. Mean = 12, median = 12, dotplot is roughly mirror-image about 12.
  2. Annual incomes — long upper tail with a few very high values.
  3. Test scores where most students did very well and a small group struggled.

(a) Approximately symmetric.

(b) Right-skewed (long tail to the right).

(c) Left-skewed (long tail to the left, toward the lower scores).

Problem 5 [2 pts]

Use the histogram below — the body-mass index (BMI) of 80 adult patients — to estimate the median and describe the shape. (1 pt each)

The peak is around 25-27, the upper tail stretches to the high 30s/40s, and the lower tail is short — right-skewed. The median appears to be around 27.
