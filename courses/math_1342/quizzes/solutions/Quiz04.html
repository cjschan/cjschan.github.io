Quiz 4

Regression, Probability, and Sampling Distributions

Worked Solutions

Covers Sections 2.6, P.1, 3.1, 3.2

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Total: 10 points

Problem 1 [2 pts]

A least-squares regression of exam score on hours studied gives \(\hat{y} = 50 + 4x\), where \(x\) = hours studied. (1 pt each)

  1. Interpret the slope in context.
  2. Predict the exam score for a student who studies 6 hours. Should you use this line to predict the score for a student who studies 40 hours? Explain.

(a) For each additional hour studied, the predicted exam score increases by 4 points.

(b) \(\hat{y} = 50 + 4(6) = 74\). Predicting at 40 hours would be extrapolation far outside the data — unreliable.

Problem 2 [2 pts]

A jar holds 10 red, 6 blue, and 4 green marbles. Drawing one at random, find:

  1. \(P(\text{red})\)
  2. \(P(\text{red or green})\)
  3. If two marbles are drawn without replacement, find \(P(\text{both blue})\).

Total = 20. (a) \(10/20 = 0.5\).

(b) Disjoint events: \(10/20 + 4/20 = 14/20 = 0.7\).

(c) \(\dfrac{6}{20} \cdot \dfrac{5}{19} = \dfrac{30}{380} = \dfrac{3}{38} \approx 0.079\).

Problem 3 [2 pts]

The proportion of left-handed students in a population is \(p = 0.1\). For samples of size \(n = 100\):

  1. What is the center of the sampling distribution of \(\hat{p}\)? (0.5 pt)
  2. What is its standard error? Use \(SE = \sqrt{p(1-p)/n}\). (1 pt)
  3. Roughly what range of \(\hat{p}\) values would we expect (using ±2 SE)? (0.5 pt)

(a) \(p = 0.10\).

(b) \(SE = \sqrt{0.10 \cdot 0.90 / 100} = \sqrt{0.0009} = 0.030\).

(c) Roughly \(0.10 \pm 0.06\) → between 0.04 and 0.16.

Problem 4 [2 pts]

A 95% confidence interval for the mean nightly sleep time of college students is \((6.4, 7.2)\) hours.

  1. Interpret this interval in plain language. (1 pt)
  2. True or false: there is a 95% chance the population mean lies between 6.4 and 7.2. Briefly explain. (1 pt)

(a) We are 95% confident that the population mean nightly sleep time for college students is between 6.4 and 7.2 hours.

(b) False. The interval either does or doesn't contain the (fixed) parameter; the 95% refers to the long-run reliability of the procedure — 95% of all such intervals from repeated sampling capture the true mean.

Problem 5 [2 pts]

Below is a sampling distribution of \(\hat{p}\) from 1000 samples of size 50, drawn from a population with \(p = 0.6\). Use the dotplot to estimate the standard error of \(\hat{p}\). Compare it to the formula value \(\sqrt{0.6 \cdot 0.4 / 50}\). (1 pt each)

The bulk of the dots lies between roughly 0.50 and 0.70 (so the SD is about \((0.70-0.50)/4 \approx 0.05\) using a 4-SD width approximation, or read off the spread directly).

Formula: \(\sqrt{(0.6)(0.4)/50} = \sqrt{0.0048} \approx 0.0693\). The simulated SE should be close.
