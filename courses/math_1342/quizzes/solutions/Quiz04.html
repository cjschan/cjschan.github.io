Quiz 4

Sampling Distributions and Bootstrap CIs

Worked Solutions

Covers Sections 3.1, 3.2, 3.3, 3.4

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Total: 10 points

Problem 1 [2 pts]

The proportion of left-handed students in a population is \(p = 0.1\). For samples of size \(n = 100\):

  1. What is the center of the sampling distribution of \(\hat{p}\)? (0.5 pt)
  2. What is its standard error? Use \(SE = \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}}\). (1 pt)
  3. Roughly what range of \(\hat{p}\) values would we expect (using ±2 SE)? (0.5 pt)

(a) \(p = 0.10\). (b) \(SE = \sqrt{\frac{0.10 \cdot 0.90}{100}} = \sqrt{0.0009} = 0.030\). (c) Roughly \(0.10 \pm 0.06\) → between 0.04 and 0.16.

Problem 2 [2 pts]

A 95% confidence interval for the mean nightly sleep time of college students is \((6.4, 7.2)\) hours. (1 pt each)

  1. Interpret this interval in plain language.
  2. True or false: there is a 95% chance the population mean lies between 6.4 and 7.2. Briefly explain.

(a) We are 95% confident that the population mean nightly sleep time for college students is between 6.4 and 7.2 hours.

(b) False. The interval either does or doesn’t contain the (fixed) parameter; the 95% refers to the long-run reliability of the procedure.

Problem 3 [2 pts]

Briefly describe how to construct one bootstrap sample from a sample of size \(n = 50\), and explain why bootstrapping is useful when we want a confidence interval but only have one sample. (1 pt each)

To make one bootstrap sample, draw \(n = 50\) values with replacement from the original sample. Compute the statistic on the bootstrap sample.

Repeating this thousands of times produces a bootstrap distribution that approximates the sampling variability we’d see from repeated samples — without needing a formula for the standard error.

Problem 4 [2 pts]

A bootstrap distribution of the sample mean has standard error \(SE = 1.4\). The original sample mean is \(\bar{x} = 24.6\). Construct an approximate 95% confidence interval using the SE method (\(\bar{x} \pm 2 \, SE\)).

\(24.6 \pm 2(1.4) = 24.6 \pm 2.8\). Interval: \((21.8,\ 27.4)\).

Problem 5 [2 pts]

1000 bootstrap means have 2.5th percentile \(= 21.6\) and 97.5th percentile \(= 27.5\). Give the percentile-method 95% CI. Briefly compare to the SE-method CI from Problem 4. (1 + 1)

Percentile CI: \((21.6,\ 27.5)\). It is very close to the SE-method CI \((21.8,\ 27.4)\) — both methods give similar answers when the bootstrap distribution is roughly symmetric.
