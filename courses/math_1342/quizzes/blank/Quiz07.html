Quiz 7

Inference for One Proportion, One Mean, and a Difference of Proportions

Student Version

Covers Sections 6.1, 6.2, 6.3

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Total: 10 points

Problem 1 [2 pts]

In a poll of 250 voters, 140 favor the proposition. Construct an approximate 95% confidence interval for the population proportion using \(\hat{p} \pm 2 \, SE\) where \(SE = \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}}\). (2 pts)

Problem 2 [2 pts]

Test \(H_0: p = 0.5\) vs \(H_a: p > 0.5\) using the data from Problem 1. Compute the z statistic using \(z = \frac{\hat{p} - p_0}{\sqrt{p_0(1-p_0)/n}}\) and decide at \(\alpha = 0.05\) (critical \(z = 1.645\)).

Problem 3 [3 pts]

A sample of \(n = 36\) commute times has \(\bar{x} = 28\) min and \(s = 12\) min. (1.5 pts each)

  1. Construct an approximate 95% confidence interval for the mean commute time using \(\bar{x} \pm 2 \cdot \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}\).
  2. Test \(H_0: \mu = 30\) vs \(H_a: \mu \neq 30\) using \(t = \frac{\bar{x} - 30}{s/\sqrt{n}}\). Decide at \(\alpha = 0.05\) (use approximate critical \(\pm 2\)).

Problem 4 [3 pts]

In a study, 240 of 400 men say they exercise weekly, vs 270 of 500 women. Compute \(\hat{p}_1 - \hat{p}_2\) and a 95% confidence interval, using \(SE = \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}_1(1-\hat{p}_1)}{n_1} + \frac{\hat{p}_2(1-\hat{p}_2)}{n_2}}\). (1 diff, 2 CI/interp)
