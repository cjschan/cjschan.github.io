Quiz 7

Inference for Means and Differences

Student Version

Covers Sections 6.2, 6.3, 6.4

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Total: 10 points

Problem 1 [2 pts]

A sample of \(n = 36\) commute times has \(\bar{x} = 28\) min and \(s = 12\) min. Construct an approximate 95% confidence interval for the mean commute time using \(\bar{x} \pm 2 \cdot \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}\). (2 pts)

Problem 2 [2 pts]

To test \(H_0: \mu = 30\) vs \(H_a: \mu \neq 30\), with the same sample as Problem 1, compute the test statistic \(t = \frac{\bar{x} - 30}{\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}}\) and state whether you reject at \(\alpha = 0.05\) (using approximate critical value \(\pm 2\)). (1 + 1)

Problem 3 [2 pts]

In a study, 240 of 400 men say they exercise weekly, vs 270 of 500 women. Compute \(\hat{p}_1 - \hat{p}_2\) and a 95% confidence interval, using \(SE = \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}_1(1-\hat{p}_1)}{n_1} + \frac{\hat{p}_2(1-\hat{p}_2)}{n_2}}\). (1 pt diff, 1 pt CI)

Problem 4 [2 pts]

Two cleaning products are compared by measuring the bacterial reduction (%): Product A had \(\bar{x}_1 = 78\), \(s_1 = 8\), \(n_1 = 25\); Product B had \(\bar{x}_2 = 71\), \(s_2 = 10\), \(n_2 = 25\). Compute the difference of means and a standard error for the difference using \(SE = \sqrt{\frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_2^2}{n_2}}\). (1 + 1)

Problem 5 [2 pts]

Use the SE from Problem 4 to test \(H_0: \mu_1 = \mu_2\) vs \(H_a: \mu_1 \neq \mu_2\). Compute \(t = \frac{\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2}{SE}\) and decide at \(\alpha = 0.05\) (use approximate critical \(\pm 2\)). (1 + 1)
