Quiz 4

Sampling Distributions and Bootstrap CIs

Student Version

Covers Sections 3.1, 3.2, 3.3

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Total: 10 points

Problem 1 [2 pts]

The dotplot below shows a simulated sampling distribution of the sample proportion \(\hat{p}\) of left-handed students, built from 500 random samples of size \(n = 100\). Read the reported statistics off the plot — do not use a formula.

  1. What is the center of this sampling distribution of \(\hat{p}\)? (0.5 pt)
  2. What is the standard error of \(\hat{p}\)? (0.5 pt)
  3. Using the reported mean and standard error, roughly what range of plausible values for \(\hat{p}\) should we expect? Use ±2 standard errors. (1 pt)

Problem 2 [2 pts]

A 95% confidence interval for the mean nightly sleep time of college students is \((6.4, 7.2)\) hours. (1 pt each)

  1. Interpret this interval in plain language.
  2. True or false: there is a 95% chance the population mean lies between 6.4 and 7.2. Briefly explain.

Problem 3 [2 pts]

Briefly describe how to construct one bootstrap sample from a sample of size \(n = 50\), and explain why bootstrapping is useful when we want a confidence interval but only have one sample. (1 pt each)

Problem 4 [2 pts]

A bootstrap distribution of the sample mean has standard error \(SE = 1.4\). The original sample mean is \(\bar{x} = 24.6\). Construct an approximate 95% confidence interval using the SE method (\(\bar{x} \pm 2 \, SE\)).

Problem 5 [2 pts]

A researcher takes a random sample of 60 college students and finds a sample mean study time of 14.2 hours per week. She constructs a 95% confidence interval and gets \((12.8,\ 15.6)\). Her colleague takes a different random sample of 200 students from the same population. (1 pt each)

  1. Would you expect the colleague’s 95% confidence interval to be wider, narrower, or the same width? Explain.
  2. Would you expect the colleague’s confidence interval to have the same center? Explain.
