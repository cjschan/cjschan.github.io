Quiz 8

Paired Differences in Means

Student Version

Covers Sections 6.5

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Total: 10 points

Problem 1 [2 pts]

For each design, decide whether you should use a paired or an independent two-sample approach. (1 pt each)

  1. 30 patients' blood pressure measured before and after a treatment.
  2. Cholesterol levels of 30 patients on Drug A vs 30 different patients on Drug B.

Problem 2 [2 pts]

How does inference for a paired difference in means differ from inference for the difference of two independent means? Address both the data structure (1 pt) and the statistic / standard error you compute (1 pt).

Problem 4 [3 pts]

Using the values from Problem 3 (\(n = 8\), \(\bar{d} \approx 0.79\), \(s_d \approx 0.34\)):

  1. Construct an approximate 95% confidence interval for the mean improvement using \(\bar{d} \pm 2 \cdot s_d / \sqrt{n}\). (1.5 pts)
  2. Test \(H_0: \mu_d = 0\) vs \(H_a: \mu_d > 0\) by computing the test statistic \(t = \bar{d}/(s_d/\sqrt{n})\) and stating the conclusion at \(\alpha = 0.05\) (treat the rough critical value as \(t \approx 1.9\)). (1.5 pts)
