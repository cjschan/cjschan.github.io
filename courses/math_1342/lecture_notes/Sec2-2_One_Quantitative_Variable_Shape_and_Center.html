Section 2.2

One Quantitative Variable

Shape and center: dotplots, histograms, mean, and median.

Dotplot and Histogram

Two common ways to visualize one quantitative variable.

Dotplot

  • Each case is represented by a single dot.
  • Dots are stacked when values repeat.
  • Good for small datasets where you want to see every value.

Histogram

  • The x-axis is divided into ranges (bins).
  • The height of each bar is the count of cases in that range.
  • Good for larger datasets to see the overall shape.
Histogram vs. bar chart: A bar chart is for categorical data (no numeric scale). A histogram is for quantitative data (numeric x-axis, bars touch).

Shape of a Distribution

The shape tells us about the pattern of data values.

Symmetric
Left and right sides are roughly mirror images.
Left-Skewed
Long tail stretches to the left (toward smaller values).
Right-Skewed
Long tail stretches to the right (toward larger values).

The Mean

The arithmetic average: add all values and divide by the number of cases.

Formula

\[ \bar{x} = \frac{x_1 + x_2 + \cdots + x_n}{n} = \frac{\sum x_i}{n} \]
\(n\) is the sample size and \(x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n\) are the data values.

Interpretation

  • The mean is the balance point of the distribution.
  • It uses every data value in the calculation.
  • Notation: sample mean \(\bar{x}\), population mean \(\mu\).

The Median

The middle value when data are ordered from smallest to largest.

How to Find It

  • Order all values from smallest to largest.
  • If \(n\) is odd, the median is the middle value.
  • If \(n\) is even, the median is the average of the two middle values.

Mean vs. Median and Skewness

  • The mean is pulled in the direction of skewness.
  • Right-skewed: mean > median.
  • Left-skewed: mean < median.
  • Symmetric: mean \(\approx\) median.

Outliers and Resistance

An outlier is a value notably distinct from the rest of the data.

Outliers

  • Values that are much larger or much smaller than the bulk of the data.
  • Could be a data entry mistake or a genuinely unusual observation.
  • Always investigate outliers before removing them.

Resistance

  • A statistic is resistant if it is relatively unaffected by extreme values.
  • The median is resistant to outliers.
  • The mean is not resistant to outliers.

What to Remember

Visualize, then summarize the center.

Visualization

  • Dotplot: see each case
  • Histogram: see overall shape
  • Shape: symmetric, skewed left, or skewed right

Measures of Center

  • Mean \(\bar{x}\): balance point, not resistant
  • Median \(m\): middle value, resistant
  • Mean is pulled toward skewness
