Section 2.3

Measures of Spread

Standard deviation, z-scores, the five number summary, and the 95% rule.

Standard Deviation

A measure of how spread out the data are around the mean.

Formula

\[ s = \sqrt{\frac{\sum (x_i - \bar{x})^2}{n - 1}} \]
Sample standard deviation: \(s\). Population standard deviation: \(\sigma\).

Interpretation

  • Gives a rough estimate of the typical distance from the mean.
  • Larger SD = more spread out data.
  • Smaller SD = data clustered near the mean.

The 95% Rule

For approximately bell-shaped distributions, about 95% of data falls within two standard deviations of the mean.

The Rule

\[ \text{95% of data falls between } \mu - 2\sigma \text{ and } \mu + 2\sigma \]
This only applies when the distribution is roughly symmetric and bell-shaped.

Using the Rule

  • If \(\mu = 100\) and \(\sigma = 15\), then about 95% of values are between 70 and 130.
  • Values outside this range are unusual.

Z-Scores

A z-score measures how many standard deviations a value is from the mean.

Formula

\[ z = \frac{x - \text{mean}}{\text{SD}} \]
Positive z: above the mean. Negative z: below the mean.

Comparing Across Scales

Which is better: ACT of 28 or SAT of 2100?
  • ACT: \(\mu=21, \sigma=5\). \(z = (28-21)/5 = 1.4\)
  • SAT: \(\mu=1500, \sigma=325\). \(z = (2100-1500)/325 \approx 1.85\)
  • SAT score is more impressive (higher z-score).

Five Number Summary and Quartiles

Five values that capture the spread and center of a distribution.

Five Number Summary

  • Minimum: smallest value
  • Q1: 25th percentile (median of lower half)
  • Median: 50th percentile
  • Q3: 75th percentile (median of upper half)
  • Maximum: largest value

Percentiles

  • The \(P\)th percentile is the value greater than \(P\%\) of the data.
  • Q1 = 25th percentile, Median = 50th, Q3 = 75th.

Range and IQR

Two more measures of spread with different properties.

Definitions

\[ \text{Range} = \text{Max} - \text{Min} \]
\[ \text{IQR} = Q_3 - Q_1 \]

Resistance

  • Range: not resistant (affected by outliers)
  • IQR: resistant (not affected by outliers)
  • Standard deviation: not resistant

What to Remember

Use mean and SD together, or use median and IQR together.

Not Resistant

  • Mean
  • Standard deviation
  • Range
Use all the data, so they carry the most information, but are affected by outliers.

Resistant

  • Median
  • IQR
Not affected by extreme values, so better when outliers are present.
