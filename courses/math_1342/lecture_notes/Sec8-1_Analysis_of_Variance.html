Section 8.1

Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)

ANOVA tests for a difference in means across more than two groups by comparing between-group and within-group variability.

Why We Need ANOVA

A two-sample t test compares two means. ANOVA extends inference to three or more groups.

Situation

  • Response variable is quantitative.
  • Explanatory variable defines \(k\) groups.
  • We want to test whether all group means are equal.

Hypotheses

\[ H_0:\mu_1=\mu_2=\cdots=\mu_k \]
\[ H_a:\text{at least one population mean differs} \]

Main Idea Behind ANOVA

Evidence for different means gets stronger when the groups are far apart relative to the variation inside each group.

Between
How far the group means are from the overall mean.
Within
How much individual observations vary around their own group means.
Compare
A large ratio of between to within variability supports \(H_a\).

Notation and Sums of Squares

ANOVA breaks total variability into two pieces.

Notation

  • \(k\) = number of groups
  • \(n_i\) = sample size in group \(i\)
  • \(n\) = total sample size
  • \(\bar{x}_i\) = group mean
  • \(\bar{x}\) = overall mean

Decomposition

\[ SS_{Total} = SSG + SSE \]
\[ SSG = \sum_{i=1}^{k} n_i(\bar{x}_i-\bar{x})^2 \] \[ SSE = \sum_{i=1}^{k}\sum_{j=1}^{n_i}(x_{ij}-\bar{x}_i)^2 \]
You do not need to memorize these formulas; focus on what SSG and SSE measure.
  • \(SSG\): variability due to differences among group means
  • \(SSE\): variability inside the groups
In plain English: \(SSG\) measures how much the group averages differ from each other, while \(SSE\) measures how spread out the individual values are within each group.

Cuckoo Egg Example

This example compares mean cuckoo egg length across five host-bird species.

Question
Do mean cuckoo egg lengths differ across the five host-bird groups?
Hypotheses
\[ H_0:\mu_1=\mu_2=\mu_3=\mu_4=\mu_5 \]
In words: the population mean cuckoo egg length is the same for all five host-bird species.
\[ H_a:\text{at least one population mean differs} \]
In words: at least one host-bird species has a different population mean cuckoo egg length.

ANOVA Table Structure

The F statistic compares average variability between groups to average variability within groups.

SourcedfSSMS
Groups\(k-1\)\(SSG\)\(MSG=SSG/(k-1)\)
Error\(n-k\)\(SSE\)\(MSE=SSE/(n-k)\)
Total\(n-1\)\(SS_{Total}\)---

Test Statistic

\[ F=\frac{MSG}{MSE} \]
If the null hypothesis is true, the two mean squares should be similar, so \(F\) should be close to \(1\).

Cuckoo Egg ANOVA Numbers

Use the following sums of squares and degrees of freedom to build the ANOVA table.

SourcedfSS
Groups435.90

Mean Squares
\[ MSG=\frac{35.90}{4}=8.975 \]
\[ MSE=\frac{101.29}{115}\approx 0.881 \]

Compute the F Statistic

Now compare the two mean squares.

Calculation

\[ F=\frac{8.975}{0.881}\approx 10.19 \]
A value this large means the group means are much farther apart than we would expect from within-group noise alone.

p-Value

With numerator \(df=4\) and denominator \(df=115\), this F value gives a very small p-value, so we reject \(H_0\).
Conclusion: there is strong evidence that mean cuckoo egg length is not the same for all host-bird groups.

Conditions for Using the F Distribution

ANOVA uses an F distribution when the model assumptions are reasonable.

Shape
Each group should have a roughly normal distribution, or each sample size should be large.
Spread
Group standard deviations should be similar. A rough rule: the largest SD should not exceed twice the smallest.
Random
Observations should be independent within and across groups.

What ANOVA Tells You

A significant ANOVA says at least one mean differs, but it does not identify all pairwise differences by itself.

What You Learn

  • There is evidence that not all population means are equal.
  • The overall group effect is statistically significant.

What You Still Need

  • Graphs or follow-up comparisons to see which groups differ.
  • Context to decide whether the difference is practically important.

What to Remember

ANOVA tests for a difference in means by comparing two kinds of variability.

Core Formulas

\[ SS_{Total}=SSG+SSE \]
\[ F=\frac{SSG/(k-1)}{SSE/(n-k)} \]

Core Ideas

  • Large between-group variability helps reject \(H_0\).
  • The p-value comes from an F distribution.
  • Need similar spreads, independent observations, and roughly normal groups or large samples.
