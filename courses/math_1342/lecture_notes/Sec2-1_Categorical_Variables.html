Section 2.1

Categorical Variables

Summarizing and visualizing categorical data with frequency tables, proportions, and bar charts.

One Categorical Variable

A frequency table counts the number of cases in each category.

Frequency Table: Cell Phones

TypeCount
Android458
iPhone437
Blackberry141
Non-Smartphone924
No Cell Phone293
Total2253

What We Learn

  • The most common category is Non-Smartphone.
  • We can convert counts to proportions or percentages.
  • A relative frequency table shows proportions instead of counts.

Proportion

A proportion summarizes a categorical variable as a single number between 0 and 1.

Formula

\[ \hat{p} = \frac{\text{count in category}}{\text{total count}} \]
Example: proportion with no cell phone:
\[ \hat{p} = \frac{293}{2253} \approx 0.130 \]

Key Points

  • Proportions and percentages are interchangeable.
  • \(0.130 = 13.0\%\)
  • All proportions in a relative frequency table sum to 1.

Visualizations: One Categorical Variable

Bar charts and pie charts display the distribution of a categorical variable.

Bar Chart

  • The height of each bar equals the count (or proportion) in that category.
  • Categories are on the x-axis.
  • Bars do not touch (unlike histograms).

Pie Chart

  • The area of each slice corresponds to the proportion in that category.
  • All slices sum to 100%.
  • Best when there are only a few categories.

Two Categorical Variables

A two-way table shows counts for every combination of two categorical variables.

Relationship Status by Gender

In a Relationship321042
It's Complicated12719
Single6345108
Total10762169

Reading the Table

  • Proportion in a relationship: \(42/169 \approx 25\%\)
  • Proportion of females in a relationship: \(32/107 \approx 30\%\)
  • Proportion of males in a relationship: \(10/62 \approx 16\%\)

Proportions from Two-Way Tables

The denominator changes depending on the question you are asking.

Different Questions, Different Denominators

  • Proportion of all students in a relationship: \(42/169\)
  • Proportion of females in a relationship: \(32/107\)
  • Proportion of people in a relationship who are female: \(32/42\)

Caution

The proportion of females in a relationship (30%) is not the same as the proportion of people in a relationship who are female (76%).

Visualizations: Two Categorical Variables

Side-by-side and segmented bar charts show how categories compare across groups.

Side-by-Side Bar Chart

  • Bars for each group are placed next to each other.
  • Easy to compare counts across groups.

Segmented Bar Chart

  • Bars are stacked instead of side-by-side.
  • Shows how each group breaks down into categories.
Summary statistic: The difference in proportions compares a categorical outcome across two groups.

Example: Bar Chart

Frequencies of favorite ice cream flavors among 200 surveyed students.

What to Remember

Tools for summarizing categorical data.
