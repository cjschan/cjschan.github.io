Course Content

MATH 1342 — Elementary Statistics

Credit Hours: 4 · Contact Hours: 4 · (3-3-0)

A first course in statistics for students in business; nursing; allied health; or the social, physical, or behavioral sciences. Topics include frequency distributions, graphing, measures of location and variation, the binomial and normal distributions, z-scores, t-test, chi-square test, F-test, hypothesis testing, analysis of variance, regression, and correlation.

NCBM 0142 — Support for Elementary Statistics

(1-1-0) · Not for college level credit

Designed to support students concurrently enrolled in MATH 1342 by providing additional focus on MATH 1342 topics and just-in-time review of prerequisite topics, as needed.

MATH 1342 Course Rationale

Students will learn to:

  • Determine the aspects of a question for which statistics can provide relevant information.
  • Analyze statistical studies, particularly regarding sampling and experimental design.
  • Select and use appropriate statistical analyses to get useful information from data.
  • Communicate knowledge using standard statistical language and interpret it in non-technical language.

Meets the Core Curriculum requirement in mathematics and the introductory statistics requirement for many majors.

NCBM 0142 Outcomes

  • Increase / improve statistical literacy.
  • Communicate mathematical ideas in a small group setting.
  • Just-in-time review of prerequisite arithmetic and algebra topics.

MATH 1342 Course Objectives

  • Interpret population vs. sample, random variables, and descriptive statistics (frequency distributions, histograms, boxplots, scatterplots).
  • Calculate and interpret measures of central tendency and dispersion (mean, median, standard deviation, quartiles).
  • Use empirical probabilities in bootstrap and randomization distributions for confidence intervals and hypothesis tests.
  • Use theoretical probabilities from normal, t, chi-squared, and F distributions; apply the 95% rule.
  • Analyze relationships between two quantitative variables using correlation and linear regression.
  • Analyze two-way tables for relationships between categorical variables.
  • Apply sampling techniques and experimental design to data production.
  • Use sampling distributions of sample proportions and means.
  • Estimate single and difference of means/proportions with confidence intervals.
  • Hypothesis test for means and proportions, single and two-population.
  • Hypothesis test using chi-squared tests.
  • Inference for regression and ANOVA.

Students use statistical applets for computations and graphical displays, with some calculations done by hand on a scientific calculator.

MATH 1342 Student Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion, a student should be able to:

  • Explain the use of data collection and statistics as tools to reach reasonable conclusions.
  • Recognize, examine, and interpret the basic principles of describing and presenting data.
  • Compute and interpret empirical and theoretical probabilities using probability rules and combinatorics.
  • Explain the role of probability in statistics.
  • Examine, analyze, and compare sampling distributions for discrete and continuous random variables.
  • Describe and compute confidence intervals.
  • Solve linear regression and correlation problems.
  • Perform hypothesis testing using statistical methods.
