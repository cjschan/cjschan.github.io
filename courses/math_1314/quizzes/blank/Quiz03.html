Quiz 3

Linear Inequalities and Modeling

Student Version

Covers Sections 2.3, 2.4

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Problem 1

Solve and write the answer in interval notation.

  1. \(2x - 7 \leq 5\)
  2. \(3 - 4x > 19\)

Problem 2

Solve the three-part inequality \(-5 \leq 3x + 1 < 7\). Write the answer in interval notation.

Problem 3

Solve and graph each on a number line.

  1. \(x - 4 < -1\) and \(2x + 3 \geq 1\)
  2. \(3x > 6\) or \(x + 5 \leq 4\)

Problem 4

The pressure \(P\) (in atm) of a sample of gas at constant temperature varies directly with the depth \(d\) (in feet) below the surface of a lake. At 33 ft the pressure is 2 atm.

  1. Find the constant of variation.
  2. What is the pressure at 99 ft?

Problem 5

A rideshare company charges a \$3 base fare plus \$1.50 per mile. After 5 miles you owed \$10.50, after 10 miles you owed \$18.

  1. Verify that a linear model fits this rate structure.
  2. Write a linear function \(C(m)\) for the cost.
  3. If the bill was \$24, how many miles did you ride?

Problem 6

Let \(\displaystyle f(x) = \begin{cases} 3 - x & \text{if } x < 0 \\ x^2 + 1 & \text{if } 0 \leq x \leq 3 \\ 2x & \text{if } x > 3 \end{cases}\). Evaluate \(f(-2),\; f(0),\; f(2),\; f(3),\; f(4)\).
