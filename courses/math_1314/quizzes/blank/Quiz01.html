Quiz 1

Functions, Notation, and Rate of Change

Student Version

Covers Sections 1.3, 1.4

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Problem 1

Let \(f(x) = -3x + 7\). Evaluate.

  1. \(f(2)\)
  2. \(f(-4)\)
  3. \(f(0)\)

Problem 2

The function \(g\) is given by the ordered pairs \(\{(-3, 5), (-1, 2), (0, 5), (4, -1), (7, 2)\}\). Find the domain and range of \(g\).

Problem 3

Express each set in both interval notation and set-builder notation.

  1. All real numbers \(x\) with \(x > -2\).
  2. All real numbers from 1 (included) to 6 (not included).
  3. All real numbers \(x\) with \(x \leq 0\) or \(x \geq 4\).

Problem 4

Decide whether each graph represents \(y\) as a function of \(x\) using the vertical line test.

  1. A line with negative slope.
  2. A circle of radius 4 centered at \((1, 2)\).
  3. A parabola opening to the left with vertex at \((3, 0)\).

Problem 5

Find the slope of the line through each pair of points.

  1. \((-1, 4)\) and \((3, -2)\)
  2. \((5, 6)\) and \((5, -1)\)
  3. \((0, 7)\) and \((4, 7)\)

Problem 6

Find the average rate of change of \(f(x) = x^2 - 2x\) on the interval from \(x = 1\) to \(x = 4\).
