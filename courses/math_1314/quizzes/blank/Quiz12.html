Quiz 12

Three-Variable Systems and Matrix Solutions

Student Version

Covers Sections 6.3, 6.4

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Total: 10 points

Problem 1 [3 pts]

Solve the system by elimination.

\(\begin{cases} x + y + z = 6 \\ 2x - y + z = 3 \\ x + 2y - z = 5 \end{cases}\)

Problem 2 [3 pts]

Use Gaussian elimination on the augmented matrix to solve.

\(\begin{cases} x + 2y - z = 3 \\ 2x - y + z = 5 \\ x + y + z = 6 \end{cases}\)

Problem 3 [2 pts]

After row-reducing an augmented matrix, the bottom row becomes \([0\ \ 0\ \ 0 \,|\, 5]\). What does this say about the original system, and how many solutions does it have?

Problem 4 [2 pts]

A snack stand sells popcorn, candy, and soda. On a busy day they sold 80 items totaling \$200. Popcorn is \$3, candy is \$2, and soda is \$2.50. Twice as many popcorns were sold as candy. Set up a 3-variable system to model this — you do not need to solve it.
