Quiz 9

Combining Functions and Inverses

Worked Solutions

Covers Sections 5.1, 5.2

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Total: 10 points

Problem 1 [3 pts]

Let \(f(x) = x^2 - 4\) and \(g(x) = x + 5\). Find each. (1 pt each)

  1. \((f + g)(2)\)
  2. \((fg)(x)\) (factored form is fine)
  3. \(\left(\dfrac{g}{f}\right)(x)\), stating any domain restrictions

(a) \(f(2) = 0\), \(g(2) = 7\). Sum \(= 7\).

(b) \((fg)(x) = (x^2 - 4)(x + 5)\).

(c) \(\dfrac{x + 5}{x^2 - 4}\), domain \(x \neq \pm 2\).

Problem 2 [2 pts]

Let \(f(x) = 3x - 1\) and \(g(x) = x^2 + 2\). Find \((f \circ g)(x)\) and \((g \circ f)(2)\). (1 pt each)

\((f \circ g)(x) = 3(x^2 + 2) - 1 = 3x^2 + 5\).

\(f(2) = 5\); \((g \circ f)(2) = g(5) = 27\).

Problem 3 [2 pts]

Decide whether each is one-to-one. Justify briefly. (1 pt each)

  1. \(f(x) = 2x - 7\)
  2. \(g(x) = x^2 + 1\)

(a) Linear with non-zero slope. One-to-one.

(b) \(g(2) = g(-2) = 5\). Not one-to-one.

Problem 4 [3 pts]

Find the inverse of \(f(x) = \dfrac{4x - 3}{2}\) and verify by composition. (2 pts inverse, 1 pt verification)

Set \(y = \dfrac{4x - 3}{2}\), swap and solve: \(2x = 4y - 3\), \(y = \dfrac{2x + 3}{4}\). \(f^{-1}(x) = \dfrac{2x + 3}{4}\).

Check: \(f(f^{-1}(x)) = \dfrac{4 \cdot \frac{2x + 3}{4} - 3}{2} = \dfrac{2x + 3 - 3}{2} = x\) ✓.
