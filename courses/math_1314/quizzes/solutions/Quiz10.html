Quiz 10

Exponential and Logarithmic Functions

Worked Solutions

Covers Sections 5.3, 5.4

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Problem 1

Evaluate without a calculator.

  1. \(\log_2 64\)
  2. \(\log_5 \dfrac{1}{125}\)
  3. \(\log_9 3\)
  4. \(\ln e^7\)

(a) 6 (since \(2^6 = 64\)).

(b) \(-3\).

(c) \(\tfrac{1}{2}\) (since \(9^{1/2} = 3\)).

(d) 7.

Problem 2

Use the laws of logarithms.

  1. Expand \(\log\dfrac{x^3 \sqrt{y}}{z^2}\).
  2. Combine \(3 \ln x + \tfrac{1}{2} \ln y - 2 \ln z\) as a single \(\ln\).

(a) \(3 \log x + \tfrac{1}{2} \log y - 2 \log z\).

(b) \(\ln \dfrac{x^3 \sqrt{y}}{z^2}\).

Problem 3

An investment of \$2,500 grows at 5% annual interest, compounded continuously. How long until it doubles? Round to two decimals.

\(2P = P e^{0.05 t}\), so \(\ln 2 = 0.05 t\), \(t = \dfrac{\ln 2}{0.05} \approx 13.86\) years.

Problem 4

Solve.

  1. \(5^{x + 1} = 125\)
  2. \(2 e^{3x} = 30\) (round to three decimals)

(a) \(125 = 5^3\), so \(x + 1 = 3\), \(x = 2\).

(b) Divide: \(e^{3x} = 15\). Take \(\ln\): \(3x = \ln 15 \approx 2.708\), so \(x \approx 0.903\).

Problem 5

Solve.

  1. \(\log_3 (x - 4) = 2\)
  2. \(\log (x + 1) + \log (x - 2) = 1\)

(a) \(x - 4 = 9\), so \(x = 13\).

(b) Combine: \(\log[(x + 1)(x - 2)] = 1\), so \((x + 1)(x - 2) = 10\). Expand: \(x^2 - x - 2 = 10\), \(x^2 - x - 12 = 0\), \((x - 4)(x + 3) = 0\). \(x = 4\) or \(x = -3\). Domain requires \(x > 2\), so \(x = 4\).
