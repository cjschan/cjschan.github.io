Quiz 4

Quadratic Functions and Equations

Worked Solutions

Covers Sections 3.1, 3.2

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Total: 10 points

Problem 1 [2 pts]

Find the vertex and axis of symmetry of \(g(x) = x^2 + 6x - 1\). State whether the parabola opens up or down. (1 pt vertex/axis, 1 pt direction)

\(h = -\tfrac{6}{2} = -3\); \(k = g(-3) = 9 - 18 - 1 = -10\). Vertex \((-3, -10)\); axis \(x = -3\); opens up.

Problem 2 [2 pts]

Write \(f(x) = x^2 - 8x + 11\) in vertex form by completing the square.

Half of \(-8\) is \(-4\); \((-4)^2 = 16\). \(f(x) = (x^2 - 8x + 16) - 16 + 11 = (x - 4)^2 - 5\).

Problem 3 [2 pts]

Solve by factoring: \(3x^2 + 5x - 2 = 0\).

\((3x - 1)(x + 2) = 0\). \(x = \tfrac{1}{3}\) or \(x = -2\).

Problem 4 [2 pts]

Solve using the quadratic formula: \(2x^2 + 3x - 4 = 0\). Leave answer in exact form.

\(x = \dfrac{-3 \pm \sqrt{9 + 32}}{4} = \dfrac{-3 \pm \sqrt{41}}{4}\).

Problem 5 [2 pts]

A small farm’s daily revenue (in hundreds of dollars) for selling \(p\)-dollar baskets is modeled by \(R(p) = -2 p^2 + 24 p\). What price maximizes revenue, and what is the maximum revenue? (1 pt each)

\(p = -\dfrac{24}{2(-2)} = 6\) dollars per basket. \(R(6) = -72 + 144 = 72\) (hundred dollars), i.e. \$7,200.
