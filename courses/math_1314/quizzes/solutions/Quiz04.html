Quiz 4

Quadratic Functions and Equations

Worked Solutions

Covers Sections 3.1, 3.2

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Problem 1

Find the vertex and axis of symmetry. State whether the parabola opens up or down.

  1. \(f(x) = -3(x + 2)^2 + 5\)
  2. \(g(x) = x^2 + 6x - 1\)

(a) Vertex \((-2, 5)\); axis \(x = -2\); opens down (\(a = -3\)).

(b) \(h = -\dfrac{6}{2} = -3\); \(k = g(-3) = 9 - 18 - 1 = -10\). Vertex \((-3, -10)\); axis \(x = -3\); opens up.

Problem 2

Write \(f(x) = x^2 - 8x + 11\) in vertex form by completing the square.

Half of \(-8\) is \(-4\); \((-4)^2 = 16\). \(f(x) = (x^2 - 8x + 16) - 16 + 11 = (x - 4)^2 - 5\).

Problem 3

Solve by factoring.

  1. \(x^2 - 7x + 12 = 0\)
  2. \(3x^2 + 5x - 2 = 0\)

(a) \((x - 3)(x - 4) = 0\). \(x = 3, 4\).

(b) \((3x - 1)(x + 2) = 0\). \(x = \tfrac{1}{3}, -2\).

Problem 4

Solve by the quadratic formula.

  1. \(x^2 - 4x - 1 = 0\)
  2. \(2x^2 + 3x - 4 = 0\)

(a) \(x = \dfrac{4 \pm \sqrt{16 + 4}}{2} = \dfrac{4 \pm 2\sqrt{5}}{2} = 2 \pm \sqrt{5}\).

(b) \(x = \dfrac{-3 \pm \sqrt{9 + 32}}{4} = \dfrac{-3 \pm \sqrt{41}}{4}\).

Problem 5

A small farm’s daily revenue (in hundreds of dollars) for selling \(p\)-dollar baskets is modeled by \(R(p) = -2 p^2 + 24 p\).

  1. What price maximizes revenue?
  2. What is the maximum daily revenue?

(a) \(p = -\dfrac{24}{2(-2)} = 6\) dollars per basket.

(b) \(R(6) = -72 + 144 = 72\) (hundred dollars), i.e. \$7,200.
