Problem 1

Use Gaussian elimination on the augmented matrix to solve.

\(\begin{cases} x + 2y - z = 3 \\ 2x - y + z = 5 \\ x + y + z = 6 \end{cases}\)

Augmented: \(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & -1 & 3 \\ 2 & -1 & 1 & 5 \\ 1 & 1 & 1 & 6 \end{array}\right]\). R2 → R2 − 2R1, R3 → R3 − R1:

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & -1 & 3 \\ 0 & -5 & 3 & -1 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & 3 \end{array}\right]\). R2 → \(-\tfrac{1}{5}\)R2: \(\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & -\tfrac{3}{5} & \tfrac{1}{5} \end{bmatrix}\). R3 → R3 + R2:

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & -1 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & -\tfrac{3}{5} & \tfrac{1}{5} \\ 0 & 0 & \tfrac{7}{5} & \tfrac{16}{5} \end{array}\right]\).

Back-substitute: \(z = \tfrac{16}{7}\), \(y = \tfrac{1}{5} + \tfrac{3}{5} \cdot \tfrac{16}{7} = \tfrac{7 + 48}{35} = \tfrac{55}{35} = \tfrac{11}{7}\), \(x = 3 - 2 y + z = 3 - \tfrac{22}{7} + \tfrac{16}{7} = \tfrac{15}{7}\).

Solution: \(\left(\tfrac{15}{7}, \tfrac{11}{7}, \tfrac{16}{7}\right)\).

Problem 2

Let \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & -1 \end{bmatrix}\), \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -2 \\ 0 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\). Compute.

  1. \(A + B\)
  2. \(2A - B\)
  3. \(AB\)

(a) \(\begin{bmatrix} 5 & 0 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\).

(b) \(\begin{bmatrix} 2 & 4 \\ 6 & -2 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -2 \\ 0 & 5 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -2 & 6 \\ 6 & -7 \end{bmatrix}\).

(c) Row 1 of \(A\) is \((1, 2)\). \((1)(4) + (2)(0) = 4\); \((1)(-2) + (2)(5) = 8\). Row 2: \((3)(4) + (-1)(0) = 12\); \((3)(-2) + (-1)(5) = -11\). \(AB = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 8 \\ 12 & -11 \end{bmatrix}\).

Problem 3

Find \(A^{-1}\) for \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 5 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\).

\(\det A = 6 - 5 = 1\). \(A^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -5 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\).

Problem 4

Use the inverse from Problem 3 to solve the system \(\begin{cases} 3x + 5y = 11 \\ x + 2y = 4 \end{cases}\).

\(\mathbf{x} = A^{-1}\mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -5 \\ -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 11 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 22 - 20 \\ -11 + 12 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\). Solution: \((2, 1)\).
