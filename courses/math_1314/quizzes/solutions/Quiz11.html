Quiz 11

Systems of Equations and Inequalities

Worked Solutions

Covers Sections 6.1, 6.2, 6.3

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Problem 1

Solve by substitution.

\(\begin{cases} y = -2x + 7 \\ 4x + 3y = 9 \end{cases}\)

Substitute: \(4x + 3(-2x + 7) = 9\), so \(4x - 6x + 21 = 9\), \(-2x = -12\), \(x = 6\). Then \(y = -5\). Solution: \((6, -5)\).

Problem 2

Solve by elimination.

\(\begin{cases} 3x + 4y = 10 \\ 5x - 2y = 8 \end{cases}\)

Multiply the second by 2: \(10x - 4y = 16\). Add to the first: \(13x = 26\), so \(x = 2\). From \(3(2) + 4y = 10\): \(y = 1\). Solution: \((2, 1)\).

Problem 3

Identify the corner points of the region defined by

\(\begin{cases} x + 2y \leq 12 \\ 3x + y \leq 12 \\ x \geq 0,\; y \geq 0 \end{cases}\)

and find the maximum value of \(P = 2x + 5y\) on the region.

Corners: solve pairwise.

  • \((0, 0)\)
  • \(y = 0\) and \(3x + y = 12\): \((4, 0)\)
  • \(x = 0\) and \(x + 2y = 12\): \((0, 6)\)
  • \(x + 2y = 12\) and \(3x + y = 12\): multiply second by 2 and subtract: \(x + 2y - 6x - 2y = 12 - 24\), \(-5x = -12\), \(x = \tfrac{12}{5}\). Then \(y = 12 - 3 \cdot \tfrac{12}{5} = \tfrac{24}{5}\). Corner \(\left(\tfrac{12}{5}, \tfrac{24}{5}\right)\).

Evaluate \(P\): at \((0, 0)\): 0; at \((4, 0)\): 8; at \((0, 6)\): 30; at \(\left(\tfrac{12}{5}, \tfrac{24}{5}\right)\): \(P = 2 \cdot \tfrac{12}{5} + 5 \cdot \tfrac{24}{5} = \tfrac{24}{5} + 24 = \tfrac{144}{5} = 28.8\). Max is 30 at \((0, 6)\).

Problem 4

Solve the system.

\(\begin{cases} x + y + z = 6 \\ 2x - y + z = 3 \\ x + 2y - z = 5 \end{cases}\)

Subtract eq1 from eq2: \(x - 2y = -3\). Add eq1 and eq3: \(2x + 3y = 11\). From the first relation, \(x = 2y - 3\). Substitute: \(2(2y - 3) + 3y = 11\), \(7y = 17\), \(y = \tfrac{17}{7}\). Then \(x = \tfrac{34}{7} - 3 = \tfrac{13}{7}\). From eq1: \(z = 6 - x - y = 6 - \tfrac{30}{7} = \tfrac{12}{7}\).

Solution: \(\left(\tfrac{13}{7}, \tfrac{17}{7}, \tfrac{12}{7}\right)\).
