Quiz 11

Systems of Equations and Inequalities

Worked Solutions

Covers Sections 6.1, 6.2

Instructions. This is a take-home quiz. You may use your lecture notes and online resources, but the work you submit must be your own. Show your work clearly to receive credit.

Total: 10 points

Problem 1 [2 pts]

Solve by substitution.

\(\begin{cases} y = -2x + 7 \\ 4x + 3y = 9 \end{cases}\)

Substitute: \(4x + 3(-2x + 7) = 9\), so \(-2x + 21 = 9\), \(x = 6\). Then \(y = -5\). Solution: \((6, -5)\).

Problem 2 [2 pts]

Solve by elimination.

\(\begin{cases} 3x + 4y = 10 \\ 5x - 2y = 8 \end{cases}\)

Multiply the second by 2: \(10x - 4y = 16\). Add to the first: \(13x = 26\), so \(x = 2\). From \(3(2) + 4y = 10\): \(y = 1\). Solution: \((2, 1)\).

Problem 3 [3 pts]

The shaded region below is defined by

\(\begin{cases} x + 2y \leq 12 \\ 3x + y \leq 12 \\ x \geq 0,\; y \geq 0 \end{cases}\)

Identify the corner points of the region and find the maximum value of \(P = 2x + 5y\) on the region. (2 pts corners, 1 pt max value)

Corners: \((0, 0)\), \((4, 0)\), \((0, 6)\), and \(\left(\tfrac{12}{5}, \tfrac{24}{5}\right)\) (intersection of the two slanted lines).

Evaluate \(P\) at each: 0, 8, 30, \(\tfrac{144}{5} = 28.8\). Max is 30 at \((0, 6)\).

Problem 4 [3 pts]

A coffee shop sells lattes and cappuccinos. On Saturday they sold 60 drinks for a total of \$300. Lattes cost \$4 and cappuccinos cost \$6. Set up a system of equations and find how many of each were sold.

Let \(x\) = lattes, \(y\) = cappuccinos. \(\begin{cases} x + y = 60 \\ 4x + 6y = 300 \end{cases}\)

From the first: \(y = 60 - x\). Substitute: \(4x + 6(60 - x) = 300\), so \(-2x + 360 = 300\), \(x = 30\). Then \(y = 30\). 30 lattes and 30 cappuccinos.
