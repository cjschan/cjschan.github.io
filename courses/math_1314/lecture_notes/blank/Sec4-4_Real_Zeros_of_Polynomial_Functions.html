Section 4.4

Real Zeros of Polynomial Functions

Student Notes

How do we actually find the zeros of a higher-degree polynomial? We’ll use the factor theorem, factoring techniques (including grouping and substitution), and graphs to identify zeros and factor polynomials.

Polynomial Long Division

Division Algorithm

If \(f(x)\) is divided by a non-zero polynomial \(d(x)\), there exist a unique quotient \(q(x)\) and remainder \(r(x)\) with \(f(x) = d(x) q(x) + r(x)\), where \(\deg r < \deg d\). Polynomial long division works just like long division of numbers: divide the leading terms, multiply, subtract, bring down, repeat.

Example 1

Long division

Use long division to compute \((2x^3 - 5x^2 + 3x - 4) \div (x - 2)\).

Remainder and Factor Theorems

Remainder Theorem

When \(f(x)\) is divided by \((x - c)\), the remainder equals \(f(c)\).

Factor Theorem

\((x - c)\) is a factor of \(f(x)\) if and only if \(f(c) = 0\). Conversely, every factor \((x - c)\) of \(f\) gives a zero \(x = c\).

Example 2

Verifying a zero and factoring

Show that \(x = 3\) is a zero of \(f(x) = x^3 - 4x^2 + x + 6\). Then use long division to factor \(f\) completely and find all zeros.

Zeros by Factoring

Useful Factoring Techniques
  • Common factor: pull out any GCF first.
  • Grouping: for four-term polynomials, group two pairs and factor each pair to expose a common binomial factor.
  • Substitution: for polynomials of the form \(ax^4 + bx^2 + c\) (or other “quadratic in disguise”), let \(u = x^2\) and solve the resulting quadratic.
  • Special forms: difference of squares \(a^2 - b^2 = (a-b)(a+b)\); difference/sum of cubes \(a^3 \pm b^3 = (a \pm b)(a^2 \mp ab + b^2)\).

Approximating Zeros from a Graph

When Algebra Doesn’t Factor Cleanly

Not every polynomial factors over the integers. When it doesn’t, you can still:

  • Sketch the graph and look for sign changes — each sign change locates a real zero between consecutive integers.
  • Use a graphing calculator’s zero or root tool to approximate the value.
