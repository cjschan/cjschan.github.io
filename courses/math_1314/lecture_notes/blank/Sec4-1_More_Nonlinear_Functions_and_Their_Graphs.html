Section 4.1

More Nonlinear Functions and Their Graphs

Student Notes · View worked solutions →

We zoom out from quadratics to broader features of nonlinear graphs: increasing/decreasing intervals, local extrema, absolute extrema, and even/odd symmetry. These ideas apply to any function and become essential vocabulary for the rest of the course.

Local and Absolute Extrema

Local Extrema

A function \(f\) has a local maximum at \(x = c\) if \(f(c) \geq f(x)\) for all \(x\) in some open interval around \(c\). Similarly for a local minimum with \(\leq\). The output \(f(c)\) is the local max/min value.

Absolute (Global) Extrema

An absolute maximum is the largest output \(f\) attains anywhere in its domain. An absolute minimum is the smallest. A function may have neither (e.g., a line).

Increasing and Decreasing

Reading Behavior

On any interval where the graph is rising left-to-right, the function is increasing. Falling means decreasing. Constant means flat. Use open intervals when reading from a graph.

Symmetry: Even and Odd Functions

Even and Odd Functions

A function \(f\) is

  • Even if \(f(-x) = f(x)\) for every \(x\) in the domain. Its graph is symmetric across the \(y\)-axis.
  • Odd if \(f(-x) = -f(x)\) for every \(x\). Its graph is symmetric about the origin (180° rotational).

Most functions are neither even nor odd.

Example 3

Testing for symmetry

  1. \(f(x) = x^4 - 3x^2\)
  2. \(g(x) = x^3 - x\)
  3. \(h(x) = x^2 + x\)
  4. \(k(x) = \dfrac{1}{x}\)

Continuity at a Glance

Continuous Function

Informally, a function is continuous on an interval if its graph can be drawn over that interval without lifting the pencil. Functions with breaks, jumps, holes, or vertical asymptotes are discontinuous there.
