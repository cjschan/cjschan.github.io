Section 6.1

Systems of Linear Equations in Two Variables

Student Notes

A system of two linear equations in two variables describes the intersection of two lines. We solve such systems graphically, by substitution, and by elimination, and apply the methods to short word problems.

Three Possibilities

Solution Cases

Two lines in the plane satisfy exactly one of:

  • Intersect at one point — consistent and independent. One unique solution.
  • Coincide (same line) — consistent and dependent. Infinitely many solutions.
  • Parallel and distinct — inconsistent. No solution.

Substitution Method

Example 1

Substitution

Solve the system.

\(\begin{cases} y = 3x - 2 \\ 2x + y = 8 \end{cases}\)

Elimination Method

Strategy
  1. Multiply one or both equations by suitable constants so a variable cancels when you add the equations.
  2. Add the equations to eliminate that variable.
  3. Solve for the remaining variable.
  4. Back-substitute to find the other.
