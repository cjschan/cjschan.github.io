Section 3.1

Quadratic Functions and Models

Student Notes

A quadratic function has a degree-2 polynomial as its formula. Its graph is a parabola. Today we identify the vertex, axis of symmetry, and \(x\)-intercepts, switch between standard and vertex form, and use the vertex to solve maximum/minimum problems.

Quadratic Functions

Quadratic Function

A quadratic function can be written in standard form \[ f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c, \] with \(a \neq 0\). Its graph is a parabola that opens upward when \(a > 0\) and downward when \(a < 0\).

Key Features of a Parabola
  • Vertex: the lowest point if the parabola opens up, the highest if it opens down.
  • Axis of symmetry: the vertical line through the vertex.
  • \(y\)-intercept: \((0, c)\) (set \(x = 0\)).
  • \(x\)-intercepts: the solutions of \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\), if any.

Vertex Form

Vertex Form

A quadratic in vertex form is \[ f(x) = a(x - h)^2 + k. \] The vertex is \((h, k)\) and the axis of symmetry is \(x = h\).

Example 1

Reading off the vertex

For each function, identify \(a\), the vertex, the axis of symmetry, and whether the parabola opens up or down.

  1. \(f(x) = 2(x - 3)^2 - 5\)
  2. \(g(x) = -\tfrac{1}{2}(x + 1)^2 + 4\)
  3. \(h(x) = (x + 6)^2\)

Vertex from Standard Form

Vertex Formula

For \(f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c\), the \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex is \[ h = -\frac{b}{2a}, \] and the \(y\)-coordinate is \(k = f(h)\). The axis of symmetry is \(x = h\).

  2. \(g(x) = -2x^2 - 8x + 1\)

Maximum and Minimum Applications

Max/Min from the Vertex
  • If \(a > 0\), the parabola opens up and \(k\) is the minimum output.
  • If \(a < 0\), the parabola opens down and \(k\) is the maximum output.
  • The optimum is achieved at \(x = h\).
Example 5

Maximum revenue

A small theater finds that ticket revenue is modeled by \(R(p) = -50 p^2 + 600 p\), where \(p\) is the ticket price (dollars). What price maximizes revenue, and what is the maximum revenue?

Example 6

Maximum height of a projectile

A ball is thrown upward from a 96-ft cliff with initial velocity 80 ft/s. Its height in feet after \(t\) seconds is \(h(t) = -16 t^2 + 80 t + 96\).

  1. How many seconds until the ball reaches maximum height?
  2. What is that maximum height?
